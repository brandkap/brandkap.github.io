DatePicker

Schedule aperiodic events with ease.

DatePicker is an event planner that serves as an extension to Google Calendar. DatePicker contains tools for customizable date selection and group collaboration to streamline event planning.

Role

UI/UX

Duration

4 weeks

Team

3 Developers
1 UX designer

Why do we need another calendar tool?

Scheduling aperiodic events on Google Calendar is very difficult to do. When a user wants to schedule an event that falls under an inconsistent pattern of days, or aperiodicly, Google Calendar does not support a fast way to do this. Even in the custom recurrence menu, your event must fall under consistent days across one or multiple weeks. Coordinating schedules across the district level, school level, department level is a hassle for school staff. In this case, our stakeholder, who is a teacher and basketball coach, has to frequently coordinate sporting events at various levels with distinct dates and frequencies.

In the image below, we can see that when creating a reoccuring event in Google Calendar it must land on consistent repeating days. Scheduling an event on MWF on one week and then TWF on the next week has to be done completely separately. This inability to schedule events in an aperiodic format causes major delays in event planning.

The solution

Provide a way to schedule custom events while collaborating with groups. In addition to this, editing past made events with changes notifications being pushed to th egroups associcated with that events. The goal for DatePicker serves to be a stripped down version of Google Calendar with increased customization options for date selection. DatePicker pushes all added events to the users connected Google account.

User interviews & feedback

User interviews and surveys were conducted to gain understanding for how teachers and coaches use their current calendars. This guided the planning phase and brainstorming of features. Based on what I learned I identified a few key tasks: ‍ Choose dates for an event Edit any past made events Share an event with a group of contacts Assign a group/groups to an event ‍ With these task outlined there were two high level design goals: 1. Design all the basic functionalities of a calendar for mobile format 2. Enchance on the basics to support aperiodic events and rapid collaboration.

These surveys and interviews helped determine what aspects of event planning are essential for DatePicker and which are secondary.

Confirming dates

The final step in the flow for scheduling an event is to confirm the dates. Rather than make the user remember the event name and times chosen in the previous screen while selecting dates, that information next to the button for confirming selected dates. User testing was done with 5 particicpants for several iterations of a confirmation button.

The three main areas in question were:

  • Button clarity
  • Where to place the button
  • Shape of container for the info and button

Receiving feedback and observing people interact with all the iterations showed that:

  • Option A's button resulted in awkward positions for scrolling through the calendar and accidental clicks
  • The buttons for Option B and D did not immediately strike people as something that was clickable
  • Options C and E provided the best balance between location, touch area size for easy clickability,allowing space for natural scrolling finger movement, and button size and clarity,

With Option C and E being very similar, I ended up moving foward with Option E because the circular pill shape provided more visual contrast amongst the grid calendar shape.

How we can give full control over the date selection process?

This is the most important feature of DatePicker. Allowing fully customizable patterns of date selection solves the biggest pain point when using Google Calendar. The goal was to allow total control over the date selection process in a way that does not have have to adhere to a specific pattern of days. This was done by implementing h a “click and pick” type selector. Simply click the dates in any frequency or pattern to schedule an event on that day.
