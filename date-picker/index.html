DatePicker

Schedule aperiodic events with ease.

DatePicker

Role

UX Design

Duration

Dec 2019 - Feb 2020

Team

Problem

Scheduling aperiodic events on Google calendars takes forever

There is one specific area on google calendars that causes a lot of trouble for those scheduling events. When a user wants to schedule an event that falls under an inconsistent pattern of days, or aperiodicly, google calendars does not support a fast way to do this. DatePicker is not meant to replace Google calendars, but to serve as an tool to solve a very specific pain point that people have with scheduling aperiodic events in google calendars. You cannot click and choose each day on the main calendar view i ngoogle calendars. You have to schedule an event that falls under a consistent frquency. Even in the custom recurrence menu, your event must fall under consistent days across multiple weeks.

In the image below, we can see that when creating a reoccuring event in GC it must land on consistent repeating days with a week. I cannot schedule an event on MWF on one week and the TWF on the next week. This causes major delays in scheduling an event

Discovery

How do social workers use ConnectOurKids?

By exploring features on the website and using information given to our team by the CTO we were able to identify the 3 main ways a social workers utilizes the ConnectOurKids tools:

Define

Reviewing the existing designs

For each feature of the ConnectOurKids website we made notes of the layout, UI, flows, and We made notes for ways to convert the layout to mobile and improve on the designs:

1. People search

People search is the way social workers find extended family of foster children. Potential candidates for a permanent home can be located through here.

2. Family connections

Family Connections contains the files of foster children. Social workers can log past engagements, upload files, and edit contact details for family connections.

Key features

Creating a mobile experience

Our reference for designing the mobile screens was the ConnectOurKids website. We reviewed the existing website designs, brainstormed ideas, and took notes of things we wanted to incorporate in the future mobile designs. We had to create a mobile experience that maintained the same functionality and organization as the website, while keeping an eye our for problems on the website that could be improved on a mobile experience. The two main features we worked on were People search and Family connections.

Style guide

Consistent design system

Our interface choices were based on the design elements already present on the website. We did not want to reinvent the wheel and have the apps UI vary drastically. So in order to keep the experience as similar as possible between the website and mobile app we use the same using consistent: copy, color schemes, typeface, and navigation layout.

User tesing

Validating our designs

We conducted user testing with a high fidelity prototype once we had a prototype ready we got in contact with 3 social workers to have them do certain tasks. Our participants worked in the fields of maintainging relationships with the foster children, a focus on building family connecttions, Our main goals of user tesing were to identify pain points that could be improved in future iterations, see if participants familiarity with the ConnectOurKids website transferred into easy use of the app, and get feedback on how the app would fit into the participants work flows.

Participants were given the tasks of:

  1. Search for a “john doe” in the people search
  2. Filter a family connection according to 2 filter criteria
  3. Edit the contact details of a family connection
