DatePicker

Schedule aperiodic events with ease.

DatePicker is a non-profit organization that aims to connect foster children with a permanent home. Their organization provides free tools to social workers to help expedite their work. Our goal was to take the main features of the ConnectOurKids website and create a mobile app incorporating those features while finding opportunities to improve the experience. We had weekly meetings with the CTO of ConnectOurKids.

Role

UI/UX, Wireframing, Prototyping

Duration

6 weeks

Team

3 developers, 1 designer

Problem

Scheduling aperiodic events on Google calendars takes forever

Coordinating schedules across the district level, school level, department level is difficult for school staff. In this case, our stakeholder, who is a teacher and basketball coach, has to frequently coordinate sporting events at various levels with distinct dates and frequencies. Google calendars is the main calendar used by teacher staff, but there is one specific area on Google calendars that causes a lot of trouble for those scheduling events. When a user wants to schedule an event that falls under an inconsistent pattern of days, or aperiodicly, google calendars does not support a fast way to do this. Even in the custom recurrence menu, your event must fall under consistent days across one or multiple weeks.

In the image below, we can see that when creating a reoccuring event in GC it must land on consistent repeating days. I cannot schedule an event on MWF on one week and the TWF on the next week. This causes major delays in event planning.

Discovery

User interviews & feedback

User interviews and surveys were conducted to gain understanding for how teachers and coaches use their calendars. This guided the planning phase and brainstorming features. Based on what I learned I identified a few key tasks: ‍ Choose dates for an event Edit any past made events Share an event with a group of contacts Assign a group/groups to an event ‍ With these task outlined there were two high level design goals: 1. Design all the basic functionalities of a calendar for mobile format 2. Enchance on the basics to support aperiodic events and rapid collaboration

Key features

Creating a mobile experience

Our reference for designing the mobile screens was the ConnectOurKids website. We reviewed the existing website designs, brainstormed ideas, and took notes of things we wanted to incorporate in the future mobile designs. We had to create a mobile experience that maintained the same functionality and organization as the website, while keeping an eye our for problems on the website that could be improved on a mobile experience. The two main features we worked on were People search and Family connections.

How we can give full control over the date selection process?

The goal was to allow total control over the date selection process in a way that does not have have to adhere to a specific pattern of days. This was done through a “click and pick” type selector. Simply click the dates in any frequency or pattern to schedule an event on that day.

Style guide

Consistent design system

Our interface choices were based on the design elements already present on the website. We did not want to reinvent the wheel and have the apps UI vary drastically. So in order to keep the experience as similar as possible between the website and mobile app we use the same using consistent: copy, color schemes, typeface, and navigation layout.

User tesing

Validating our designs

We conducted user testing with a high fidelity prototype once we had a prototype ready we got in contact with 3 social workers to have them do certain tasks. Our participants worked in the fields of maintainging relationships with the foster children, a focus on building family connecttions, Our main goals of user tesing were to identify pain points that could be improved in future iterations, see if participants familiarity with the ConnectOurKids website transferred into easy use of the app, and get feedback on how the app would fit into the participants work flows.

Participants were given the tasks of:

  1. Search for a “john doe” in the people search
  2. Filter a family connection according to 2 filter criteria
  3. Edit the contact details of a family connection
